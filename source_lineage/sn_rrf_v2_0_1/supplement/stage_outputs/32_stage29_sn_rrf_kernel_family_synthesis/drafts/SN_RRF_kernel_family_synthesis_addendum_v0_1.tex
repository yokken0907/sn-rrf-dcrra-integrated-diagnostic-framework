\section*{SN-RRF Kernel-Family Synthesis Addendum}

The curve-level null-refit controls and kernel-family profile suggest that SN-RRF should be described as a scale-normalized residual-response family rather than as a unique fixed-exponent law.

The locked $n = 3.258993$ kernel remains diagnostically useful and historically motivated, but neighboring monotone saturating kernels compete. In Stage 28, the locked kernel beats the per-galaxy best neighbor kernel in only $0.126$ of the eligible 143 galaxies, and the median $\Delta \mathrm{AIC}$(locked RRF $-$ best neighbor null) is $4.082$. The most frequent per-galaxy best neighbor is \texttt{saturating\_n2}, followed by \texttt{saturating\_n4}.

Therefore, the correct interpretation is not fixed-exponent uniqueness. The correct interpretation is that galaxy rotation-curve residuals appear to support a scale-normalized, monotone saturating radial response family.

\paragraph{Allowed claim.}
SN-RRF is supported as a diagnostic residual-response family under the tested protocol.
