## Interpretation

The DCRRA pipeline supports a diagnostic decomposition of dark-component-like SPARC
rotation-curve residuals. The residual population is not monolithic: response-family-like,
halo-comparator-like, mixed, nuisance-sensitive, pathology/archive, and unresolved regimes
are preserved.

This result should be stated as a residual-regime mapping result, not as a claim about the
particle identity, non-existence, or replacement of dark matter.

## Claim boundary

- No dark-matter exclusion claim.
- No Lambda-CDM replacement claim.
- No MOND/RAR falsification claim.
- No NFW-halo falsification claim.
- No Bullet-Cluster explanation claim.
- No particle-identity claim.
- No Hubble-tension solution claim.
- No SN-RRF as physical dark-matter substitute claim.
- Residual maps are diagnostic regime maps, not causal physical classifications.

## Safe manuscript-level claim

Dark-component-like galaxy rotation-curve residuals can be audited as multiple diagnostic
residual regimes rather than as a single homogeneous category. In the tested SPARC population,
a response-family-associated zone is prominent, but NFW-comparator, mixed, nuisance-sensitive,
pathology/archive, and unresolved regimes remain explicitly preserved.

## Forbidden manuscript-level claims

- Dark matter has been excluded.
- Lambda-CDM has been replaced.
- MOND/RAR has been falsified.
- NFW halos have been falsified.
- The Bullet Cluster has been explained.
- A dark-matter particle identity has been inferred.
- The Hubble tension has been solved.
- SN-RRF is a physical substitute for dark matter.
